If you live in the United States, there is a [1 in 25
chance](https://injuryfacts.nsc.org/all-injuries/preventable-death-overview/odds-of-dying/)
that you'll die of a cause that could have been avoided. That leaves a 96%
chance that your eventual demise can't be prevented by you or anyone else for
that matter. The question that preoccupies me currently is, why do we &mdash;
especially now &mdash; focus so much of our time and energy on that measily 4%?


## Predict the Unexpected

Around 2010, the entire bay area was obsessed with exponential growth. In 2020,
the rest of the world followed. 

What Silicon Valley forgot to mention to the rest of us, though, was that the 
promise of exponentiality never materialized outside of pitch decks.
